Brownie-super fudgy!

The secret to a really great brownie is using both melted chocolate AND cocoa powder, plus butter instead of oil (because butter has way better flavour than oil). The combination of these plus minimal flour, just enough to make a barely-set batter, and brown sugar rather than white (for extra moisture and chewiness) is what will deliver that perfect rich, fudgy brownie you’ve been dreaming about.
And to make a killer brownie, stir through a generous amount of chocolate chunks. Not only will you get little pockets of gooey chocolate, it also makes the brownie extra fudgy because some of the chocolate melds in with the batter as it bakes!

INGREDIENTS

  • cocoa powder
  • dark chocolate
  • baking powder
  • sugar
  • butter

RECIPE

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan with baking spray or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.
  3. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, melted butter, and eggs until well combined. Then, add the vanilla extract.
  4. Combine Dry Ingredients: In another bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined. If desired, fold in the walnuts or chocolate chips.
  5. Spread the Batter: Pour the brownie batter into the prepared baking pan, spreading it evenly to the edges.
  6. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 20 to 30 minutes, or until the top is crinkled and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs.
  7. Cool and Serve: Allow the brownies to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before slicing them into squares. Enjoy your delicious homemade brownies!
